## When to Use Generic vs. Industry-Specific Template

### Use Generic Template When:
- ✓ Job description has minimal industry-specific language
- ✓ Company operates across multiple industries
- ✓ Role is in IT/corporate function (not industry-specific)
- ✓ Can't determine industry from company website
- ✓ Industry detection algorithm confidence <0.3

### Use Industry-Specific Template When:
- ✓ Clear industry signals in job description
- ✓ Industry-specific regulations mentioned
- ✓ Domain expertise required
- ✓ Company focused on single industry
- ✓ Industry detection algorithm confidence >0.3

## Generic Template Structure

**Paragraph 1: Opening**
- Job title + company name
- Years of experience + general field
- Core value proposition (efficiency, innovation, problem-solving)

**Paragraph 2: Primary Achievement**
- Specific project or initiative
- Quantifiable outcome
- Tools/methods used
- Alignment with job requirements

**Paragraph 3: Secondary Skills**
- Collaboration or process skills
- Additional achievement or capability
- How you work (Agile, stakeholder management)

**Paragraph 4: Company Fit**
- What draws you to the company (keep generic if needed)
- How background aligns
- Work environment preference

**Paragraph 5: Closing**
- Opportunity to discuss value
- Thank you
- Forward-looking

## Common Mistakes in Generic Cover Letters

### Mistake 1: Too Generic
**Problem:** "I'm a hard worker with great communication skills"
**Fix:** "I reduced report generation time by 65%, saving 20 hours/week"

### Mistake 2: No Metrics
**Problem:** "I improved the process significantly"
**Fix:** "I improved process efficiency by 65%, resulting in $150K savings"

### Mistake 3: Vague Responsibilities
**Problem:** "I was responsible for requirements gathering"
**Fix:** "I facilitated Agile workshops with 15+ stakeholders, achieving 98% defect-free deployments"

### Mistake 4: Missing Company Research
**Problem:** "I want to work for your company"
**Fix:** "I am drawn to TechCorp's commitment to operational excellence and innovation"
(Even generic company research is better than none)

### Mistake 5: No Technical Specifics
**Problem:** "I used tools to analyze data"
**Fix:** "I analyzed data using SQL and Excel, then visualized insights in Tableau"

## Enhancing Generic Template with Research

Even with a generic template, add company-specific touches:

**Basic (No Research Possible):**
> "I am drawn to TechCorp's innovative approach and commitment to excellence."

**Better (5 min website research):**
> "I am particularly excited about TechCorp's recent expansion into cloud solutions and focus on customer-centric innovation."

**Best (15 min research - news, LinkedIn, about page):**
> "I am particularly drawn to TechCorp's mission to democratize data analytics and your recent launch of the cloud-based analytics platform, which aligns with my experience building scalable BI solutions."

## Key Takeaways for Generic Cover Letters

1. **Metrics are critical**: Without industry language, numbers prove credibility
2. **Universal competencies**: Analysis, communication, stakeholder mgmt, problem-solving
3. **Avoid fluff**: Every sentence should add value
4. **Keep it professional**: Neutral, confident tone
5. **Some research helps**: Even basic company knowledge improves fit
6. **Strong opening**: Immediately state value proposition
7. **Adaptability**: Show ability to learn and apply skills in different contexts

## When Generic Template Falls Short

**Fails for:**
- Highly regulated industries (healthcare, finance) - need compliance language
- Technical specialist roles (engineering, data science) - need technical depth
- Executive positions - need strategic vision
- Industry-specific domains with unique terminology

**In these cases:**
- Fall back to industry-specific template if possible
- Do deeper research to understand industry context
- Consult with someone in that industry
- Use a hybrid approach (generic + key industry terms)

This generic template provides a solid foundation but should be customized whenever possible with company-specific research and role-specific achievements.

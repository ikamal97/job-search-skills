## Financial Services Best Practices

### What Financial Services Cover Letters Should Include:

1. **Accuracy & Precision**
   - Exact numbers, not ranges
   - Percentages to decimal points
   - Zero-error emphasis

2. **Regulatory Awareness**
   - Name specific regulations (SOX, AML/BSA, FDIC, OCC)
   - Reference compliance, audit readiness
   - Demonstrate understanding of regulatory environment

3. **Risk Consciousness**
   - Mention controls, fraud prevention, risk management
   - Show balance between efficiency and safety
   - Reference security, privacy, data protection

4. **Audit Trail**
   - Documentation, traceability, transparency
   - Audit findings (or lack thereof)
   - Control effectiveness

5. **Conservative Tone**
   - Formal, professional language
   - Avoid startup/tech casualness
   - Emphasize trust, integrity, stability

6. **Fiduciary Language**
   - Customer trust, stakeholder responsibility
   - Integrity of financial system
   - Accuracy in handling money/data

## Red Flags to Avoid in Financial Services Cover Letters

1. **Casual language**: "I'm super excited" → "I am confident"
2. **Vague metrics**: "Improved process" → "Reduced breaks by 98%"
3. **Ignoring compliance**: Must mention regulations if applying to regulated role
4. **Typos or errors**: Zero tolerance in financial services
5. **Overpromising**: "Will revolutionize" → "Will contribute to"
6. **Missing risk awareness**: Financial services = risk management

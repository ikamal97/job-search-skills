## Roles to Avoid
- **Software Engineer / Developer roles:** Python skills are too shallow. Would fail any live coding assessment.
- **Salesforce Developer:** Admin-level only. No Apex, no Lightning development.
- **Data Analyst / Data Scientist:** SQL is basic. No statistical background, no BI tool development experience, no R/Python for analysis.
- **Senior Director / VP of Operations:** Has managed 4 people at one company. Not a "manager of managers" and doesn't have the strategic leadership experience these roles require.
- **Product Manager (technical):** No PM certification, no experience with product analytics tools (Amplitude, Mixpanel, Pendo), no experience writing product specs or managing a product roadmap. Ops-adjacent PM roles might work, but core PM roles are a stretch.
- **Roles requiring 8+ years of experience:** Has 6 years total, with 1 of those being a solo e-commerce business. Real corporate experience is 5 years.

## Authenticity Rules
- **Never claim:**
  - "Software engineer" or "developer" — Python scripting is not engineering
  - "Salesforce developer" — admin only, no code
  - "Managed a department" — managed a small team of 4 within one function
  - "Successful entrepreneur" — the business was a learning experience that didn't make money
  - "Data analyst" — can read data, cannot do real analysis
  - "Senior leader" or "executive" — was a manager, not a director or VP
  - Any proficiency in tools he's only read dashboards in (Tableau, Looker)

- **Qualify:**
  - "Python" → always add "basic scripting" or "for data processing tasks"
  - "Led a team" → specify "2 full-time coordinators + 2 part-time contractors"
  - "E-commerce experience" → always mention it was 1 year, sub-$80K revenue, and closed
  - "SQL" → always note "basic queries" — not analytical SQL
  - "Project management" → specify operational projects, no PM certification
  - "Salesforce" → always specify "administration, not development"

- **Safe to emphasize:**
  - Process design and workflow automation — this is genuinely strong, with real outcomes and metrics
  - Stakeholder facilitation and requirements gathering — 3 years of consulting proves this
  - Airtable and Zapier — legitimately proficient, built real systems that people used
  - Vendor management and contract negotiation — concrete savings with receipts
  - Employee onboarding design — built a system that cut onboarding time by 58% and is still in use
  - Written communication and documentation — years of BRDs, process docs, and client deliverables
  - Being the "glue" person who connects departments and gets cross-functional buy-in
  - Adaptability and willingness to learn — went from consulting to startup to own business and back

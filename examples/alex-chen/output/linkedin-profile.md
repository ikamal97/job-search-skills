## About Section (2,154/2,600 chars)

Most operations roles I've seen posted describe two jobs crammed into one. Half the listing is "own our tools and processes" and the other half is "be a strategic thought partner to the C-suite." I'm here for the first half. I build the systems that let product and engineering teams focus on their actual work instead of fighting their tools.

For six years, that's been my job in one form or another.

At NovaTech Solutions, a 50-person B2B SaaS startup, I ran the operational backbone for the product org. Administered Salesforce and Jira, built reporting workflows that replaced hours of manual status tracking, and coordinated cross-functional launches across four teams. When I started, the company had three disconnected project tracking systems. By the time I left, we had a single Jira instance with configured workflows, automated dashboards, and a launch checklist every team actually used.

Before that, three and a half years at Meridian Consulting Group doing process improvement for healthcare and retail clients. That taught me how to walk into an organization I know nothing about, map the workflows, find the friction, and fix it without breaking what already works. One project — redesigning vendor onboarding for a hospital network — cut the process from 22 days to 9.

I also ran a small e-commerce business for a year. It didn't make me rich, but it gave me something most ops people don't have: firsthand experience managing vendors and operations when there's nobody to escalate to.

What I bring:

- Salesforce administration (certified) and Jira configuration — building workflows and automations, not just using them
- Cross-functional coordination across Product, Engineering, CS, and Marketing without positional authority
- SQL-based reporting and dashboard creation for executive visibility
- Vendor evaluation, negotiation, and onboarding
- Python and Apps Script for light automation — enough to kill repetitive manual work, not enough to call myself an engineer

Exploring Product Operations and Business Operations roles at remote SaaS companies. If you need someone to keep the machine running: alex.chen.chi@gmail.com

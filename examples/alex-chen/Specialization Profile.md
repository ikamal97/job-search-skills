# Specialization Profile

## Professional Niche
Alex specializes in internal operations — the systems, processes, and tooling that keep a company running behind the scenes. His strongest work has been in taking messy, undocumented manual processes and turning them into structured, repeatable workflows. This spans onboarding, vendor management, expense tracking, and cross-departmental coordination.

He is not a software engineer, a data scientist, or a product manager. His value is in being the person who sits between business teams and their tools, figures out what's broken or slow, and fixes it using a combination of process design, no-code/low-code tools, and occasionally a basic script. He's at his best in companies between 30–200 people where ops is still being figured out and someone needs to own it end to end.

## Signature Projects

### NovaTech Onboarding Automation
- **Context:** When Alex joined NovaTech, new employee onboarding was a 12-day process managed through a shared Google Doc and a lot of Slack DMs. Steps were missed regularly, new hires didn't have access to tools on day one, and IT setup requests were often forgotten.
- **Role:** Designed the entire new onboarding system from scratch. Built the Airtable base, configured Zapier automations for Slack notifications and IT ticket creation, wrote the documentation, and trained department leads on the new process.
- **Complexity:** The challenge wasn't technical — it was getting 7 department leads to agree on a single process and actually use it. Had to run 4 rounds of feedback sessions and build department-specific views in Airtable to get buy-in.
- **Outcome:** Onboarding time dropped from 12 days to 5 days. The process was adopted company-wide. New hire satisfaction survey scores for "onboarding experience" went from 3.2/5 to 4.4/5 in the quarter after launch.

### Lakefront Health Claims Processing Overhaul
- **Context:** Lakefront Health (regional health insurer, ~2,000 employees) had a claims intake process that was heavily manual and full of rework. Claims adjusters were re-keying data from faxed forms, and error rates were causing downstream payment delays.
- **Role:** Led the requirements gathering phase — conducted 22 stakeholder interviews, facilitated 3 workshop sessions with claims adjusters, supervisors, and IT, and authored the 147-requirement BRD that the development team used to rebuild the workflow.
- **Complexity:** The stakeholders had conflicting views on what the process should look like. Claims adjusters wanted simplicity, supervisors wanted more audit controls, and IT wanted to minimize custom development. Spent significant time mediating between groups and finding requirements that satisfied multiple stakeholders.
- **Outcome:** The dev team built the new workflow based on the BRD. Post-implementation (Alex was no longer on the engagement when it went live), the client reported a 28% reduction in average claims processing time and a measurable decrease in re-keying errors.

### Vendor SLA Dashboard & Renegotiation
- **Context:** NovaTech had 8 third-party vendors but no systematic way to track whether they were meeting their SLAs. Performance issues were reported anecdotally in Slack, and contract renewals happened on autopilot.
- **Role:** Built an SLA tracking dashboard in Airtable, established a monthly vendor review process, and personally led 3 contract renegotiations.
- **Complexity:** The data didn't exist in one place — had to pull from email threads, support ticket logs, and invoices to construct a baseline. Some vendors pushed back hard on renegotiation.
- **Outcome:** Identified that the IT support vendor was missing SLAs 40% of the time, which had been invisible before. Replaced that vendor. Saved $34,000/year total across 3 renegotiated contracts.
